Our Mission


WATT is a non-profit dedicated to introducing girls to computer science at a young age. We aim to create a safe environment for learning and exploring at K-8 schools in the greater Seattle area. We provide strong female role models in the form of our student volunteers with a focus on girls empowering girls. seasonal ingredients.

WATT is a non-profit organization founded in 2015 by two Holy Names graduates as a way to introduce girls to computer science at a young age. We are dedicated to bringing computer science to middle school aged girls throughout the Greater Seattle Area. Our organization is run by a core team of juniors and seniors.

WATT is a non-profit organization founded by 2 Holy Names graduates, Mackenzie Jorgensen and Laura Debolt, as a way to introduce girls to computer science at a young age. Mackenzie and Laura graduated from Holy Names Academy in 2016. Both are studying Computer Science, Mackenzie at Villanova and Laura at University of Washington.

WATT is a great way to introduce girls to computer science. Being female based makes WATT an inclusive environment for girls in a field that is male dominated. WATT is focused on empowering girls while teaching them important skills in computer science.

While WATT is focused on empowering girls, especially in the STEM field, we will not turn away boys that are interested in joining. Boys that want to join should be open minded towards WATT being female based.

WATT meets once a week for six weeks. There are two sessions at every school, session A and B. Session A focuses on the fundamentals of java and game development. Session B introduces html and website development. Classes have an introduction video about computer science or women in STEM, a discussion about the video, a warm up activity to introduce the day's concept, snack, the main lesson, and a wrap up activity.

Session A introduces students to the basic fundamentals of java and how to apply those concepts in game development. Session B is centered around web development - the students will be learning how to create their own basic web page using html.
